Our History

Raghavendra Swami Math in Chhatrapati Sambhaji Nagar was established in [year] with the divine blessings of Sri Raghavendra Swami. The Math was founded by [Founder's Name] with the aim of propagating the teachings of Sri Raghavendra Swami and serving the spiritual needs of devotees in the region.

Over the years, the Math has grown from a small shrine to a significant spiritual center, attracting devotees from across Maharashtra and beyond. The Math has been instrumental in preserving and propagating the rich spiritual traditions of the Madhwa lineage and Rayaru's teachings.

Through the dedicated service of successive pontiffs and the support of countless devotees, the Math continues to thrive as a beacon of spiritual light in Chhatrapati Sambhaji Nagar.

Guru Parampara

Sri Madhvacharya (1238-1317)

The founder of Dvaita (dualistic) philosophy, who established the Ashtamathas (eight monasteries) in Udupi.

Sri Jayatirtha (1365-1388)

Known as Tikacharya, who wrote extensive commentaries on Madhvacharya's works, making them more accessible.

Sri Vyasatirtha (1460-1539)

A prominent philosopher and court scholar of the Vijayanagara Empire who greatly expanded the influence of Dvaita philosophy.

Sri Raghavendra Swami (1595-1671)

The pontiff of Mantralayam Math, known for his miracles, scholarly works, and compassion for all beings. His brindavana at Mantralayam continues to be a source of solace and miracles for devotees.

Our Mission

The mission of Raghavendra Swami Math is to:

  • Propagate the teachings and philosophy of Sri Raghavendra Swami to all sincere seekers
  • Perform daily poojas and rituals according to Vedic traditions
  • Provide spiritual guidance to devotees in their quest for inner peace and self-realization
  • Serve the community through various charitable activities like annadana, education, and healthcare
  • Preserve and promote the rich cultural and spiritual heritage of the Madhwa tradition
  • Create a spiritual sanctuary where people of all backgrounds can experience divine grace

Our Vision

Our vision is to:

  • Establish the Math as a premier center for spiritual learning and practice in Maharashtra
  • Create a vibrant community of devotees dedicated to spiritual growth and service
  • Expand our charitable activities to reach more people in need
  • Develop educational programs to teach Vedic knowledge, Sanskrit, and traditional arts
  • Build infrastructure that can accommodate growing numbers of devotees and visitors
  • Use modern technology to spread the timeless wisdom of Sri Raghavendra Swami globally
  • Foster harmony, peace, and spiritual awakening in society
